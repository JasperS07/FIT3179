World Population Analysis

This visualisation collection presents population density relation with the land area from multiple perspectives.

The "Trio" between density, land area and population

A higher population does not always mean a higher population density. Though the relationship between population and density is partial proportional, the limited land area seems to have a higher impact on density. Countries like Singapore, Monaco with a smaller land area have the highest density.

Population Distribution

The reality is the world's population is never anywhere close to "evenly distributed". For countries with more than one hundred million population, the land area and density vary drastically between continents. Russia is the only country in Europe with a large population and yet low density.

Potential Immigration?

Since the year 1960, the population of Asia has skyrocketed over the past few decades and exceeded the 4 billion mark recently. However, the total land area in Asia is only slightly larger than the second-largest continent Africa. Will the growing population and higher density calling the potential wave of immigration? We'll wait and see.
